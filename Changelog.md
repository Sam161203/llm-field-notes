# Changelog

All notable additions and updates to AI Pulse are tracked here.
Format: `[Date] Category — What was added and why it matters`

---

## April 2026

**[Apr 2026] Initial release**
- Launched AI Pulse with full coverage across LLMs, Agents, Tools, Security, Research, Datasets, Multimodal, Fine-Tuning, Inference, and Open Source Ecosystems
- Added [Fracture Lines](FRACTURE-LINES.md) as a standalone document — 9 open problems with sources and honest status assessments
- AI Security section includes attack techniques, defense tools, and standards — more depth than most AI repos give this area
- Curator's take notes added to LLMs, Agents, RAG tools, Fine-Tuning, and Inference sections

**Reasoning behind initial scope:**
> Most awesome-AI lists are fire-and-forget link dumps. This is intended to be a living document that reflects what's actually useful in 2026, not what was impressive in 2023. The Fracture Lines section exists because the field oversells its progress — someone has to track what's still genuinely broken.

## How Updates Work

Each update to this repo should have a corresponding entry here.

**Format for new entries:**

```
## [Month Year]

**[Date] Category — Short description**
- What was added
- Why it was added (is it new? better than existing options? newly relevant?)
- Optional: what it replaced or why something was removed
```

**What gets logged:**
- New model entries (when something genuinely matters, not just every release)
- New tools added to any section
- New Fracture Lines entries or status updates
- Removals (when a tool is deprecated, abandoned, or superseded)
- Major corrections

**What doesn't get logged:**
- Typo fixes
- Link updates
- Minor formatting changes
